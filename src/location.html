Rhodes Hall, one of Atlanta’s few remaining mansions on Peachtree Street, is located just north of Pershing Point. Built in 1904, prior to the development of Ansley Park, Rhodes Hall was designed by one of Atlanta’s most celebrated young architects, Willis F. Denny II, for one of the city’s wealthiest men. Constructed of Stone Mountain granite in the Romanesque Revival style, it holds statewide significance for both the quality and style of its architecture.

1516 Peachtree Rd NE, Atlanta, GA 30309
